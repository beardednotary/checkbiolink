5 Reasons Your Calendly Link is Broken (And How to Never Miss a Lead Again)

Last updated: February 2026

You just spent 3 weeks nurturing a lead. They're ready to book a $2,000 coaching package.

You never even knew they tried to book.

This is the nightmare scenario for coaches, consultants, and service providers. Your Calendly link is your conversion point. When it breaks, you lose deals.

Here are the 5 most common reasons Calendly links fail (and how to prevent it).

1. You Typo'd Your Calendly Link

What Happened:

You copied your Calendly link into your email signature, website, or Instagram bio... but you made a typo.

Common Mistakes:

  • Extra space before or after the URL
  • Missing a character: calendly.com/yournam instead of calendly.com/yourname
  • Wrong username: You changed your Calendly username but forgot to update your links

Symptoms:

  • "Page Not Found" or 404 error
  • Link redirects to Calendly homepage instead of your booking page

How to Fix:

  1. Go to your Calendly dashboard
  2. Copy your link directly from Calendly (don't type it manually)
  3. Update every place you've shared it (bio, email signature, website, etc.)

How to Prevent:

  • Use a redirect service or custom domain (like book.yourname.com) so you can change the destination URL without updating every link

2. Your Calendly Account is Paused or Suspended

What Happened:

You paused your Calendly account (maybe you were on vacation or doing maintenance), or Calendly suspended your account for terms violation.

Symptoms:

  • Link shows "This event is not available"
  • Link redirects to Calendly homepage
  • Calendar shows no available slots

How to Fix:

  1. Log into your Calendly account
  2. Go to Settings Account
  3. Check if your account is paused
  4. If suspended, contact Calendly support

How to Prevent:

  • Set up "Out of Office" or "Limited Availability" instead of pausing your entire account
  • Use buffer times and custom availability instead of turning off your calendar completely

3. Your Availability Settings Are Wrong

What Happened:

Your Calendly link works, but your calendar shows zero available time slots. Leads think you're fully booked (or the tool is broken) and leave.

Common Causes:

  • You forgot to update your availability after a schedule change
  • All your time slots got booked and you haven't opened new ones
  • Your timezone settings are wrong (you're in PST but Calendly thinks you're in EST)
  • Buffer times are too aggressive (15-min appointments with 2-hour buffers = almost no slots)

Symptoms:

  • Link loads fine
  • "No times available" message
  • Calendar shows slots in the wrong timezone

How to Fix:

  1. Go to Event Type Settings Availability
  2. Verify your working hours are correct
  3. Check your timezone settings
  4. Reduce buffer times if needed
  5. Set a longer date range (30-60 days out instead of just 7 days)

How to Prevent:

  • Review your Calendly settings monthly
  • Test your booking link yourself to see what leads see
  • Use Calendly's "Rolling Date Range" feature so new dates automatically open

4. Custom Domain or Redirect Issues

What Happened:

You're using a custom domain (like book.yourname.com) instead of calendly.com/yourname. The redirect broke.

Common Causes:

  • DNS misconfiguration
  • Domain expired and you didn't renew it
  • Redirect service (like Rebrandly or Short.io) went down
  • You changed your Calendly link but forgot to update your custom domain redirect

Symptoms:

  • book.yourname.com shows "This site can't be reached"
  • "DNS_PROBE_FINISHED_NXDOMAIN" error
  • Redirect goes to wrong Calendly page

How to Fix:

  1. Check your domain registrar (Namecheap, Google Domains, etc.)
  2. Verify your domain is active and renewed
  3. Check your CNAME or redirect settings
  4. Update the destination URL if you changed your Calendly link

How to Prevent:

  • Set domain auto-renewal
  • Use a reliable redirect service
  • Monitor your custom domain with uptime monitoring

5. Calendly Platform Outage

What Happened:

Calendly itself is down. Their servers crashed, they're doing maintenance, or there's a technical issue.

Symptoms:

  • Your link returns a server error (500, 502, 503)
  • Calendly dashboard won't load
  • Other people on Twitter are reporting the same issue

How to Fix:

  • Wait it out (usually resolves in 30 minutes to 2 hours)
  • Check Calendly's status page
  • Search Twitter for "Calendly down"

How to Prevent:

  • You can't prevent Calendly outages, but you can be notified immediately when they happen
  • Use external monitoring to alert you so you can:

1. Post on social: "Calendly is down, DM me to book"

2. Send leads to an alternate booking method

3. Not wonder why you're getting zero bookings today
