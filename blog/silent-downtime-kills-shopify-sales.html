How Shopify Stores Go Down (Without You Knowing)

1. Custom Domain DNS Issues

You're using shop.yourbrand.com instead of yourbrand.myshopify.com. One DNS misconfiguration and your custom domain stops pointing to Shopify.

Symptoms:

  • Your .myshopify.com URL still works
  • Your custom domain shows "This site can't be reached"
  • You don't notice because you always test the Shopify admin URL

Result: All your ads, social links, and SEO traffic hit a dead end.

2. Shopify Platform Outages

Rare, but it happens. Shopify goes down for maintenance or has a technical issue.

Recent example: Shopify had a 2-hour outage in October 2025 affecting thousands of stores.

Result: You can't sell. Customers can't checkout. Revenue stops.

3. Payment Gateway Failures

Your Shopify store loads fine, but Stripe, PayPal, or Shop Pay stops processing payments.

Symptoms:

  • Customers add to cart
  • Checkout page loads
  • "Payment processing error" at the final step
  • They abandon cart and never come back

Result: You see traffic but zero sales. Looks like a conversion problem when it's actually a technical failure.

4. Theme or App Conflicts

You install a new Shopify app or update your theme. Something breaks. Your product pages show a white screen.

Symptoms:

  • Homepage works fine
  • Product pages return errors
  • Customers can't view products or add to cart

Result: Traffic without sales. Again.

5. SSL Certificate Expiration

Your SSL certificate expires. Browsers show "Your connection is not private."

Symptoms:

  • Chrome/Safari shows a big red warning
  • 99% of visitors immediately bounce
  • Your ads keep running

Result: You're paying for clicks that never convert because they hit a security warning.
